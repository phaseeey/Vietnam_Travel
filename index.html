💰 Money & Payments
💵 Cash & Currency
Bring $200–300 USD in crisp, clean bills — exchange in Vietnam for best rate. Avoid airport exchanges. ATMs dispense VND (Vietnamese Dong). $1 USD ≈ 25,000 VND. Use Amex for hotel charges.
📱 Grab App
Install Grab before departure — it's the Uber/Lyft of Southeast Asia. Use for all transport in Saigon, Hanoi, and Da Nang. Pay cash or card. Always cheaper and safer than waving down a random cab.
💬 Bargaining
At markets (Ben Thanh, etc.) start at 40–50% of the asking price. It's expected and friendly. Never aggressive — always smile. If you don't want it, don't start bargaining.

⚡ Power
🔌 Vietnam Power
220V, Type A/C/F outlets. Your universal adapter covers all of these. Your Uniqlo down jacket and most electronics auto-switch voltage. Check your electric razor — must be dual-voltage (100–240V).
🌍 Jet Lag
😴 Time Difference
Vietnam (ICT) is +15 hrs ahead of Ontario, CA (PDT). Take 0.5–1mg melatonin on the plane and stay up until 10pm local time on arrival night. Sunlight exposure in the morning accelerates adjustment.

💰 Money Tips
🏧 ATMs
Use VP Bank or Vietcombank ATMs for lowest fees. Always choose to be charged in VND — never accept dynamic currency conversion (DCC) to USD. Withdraw ₫3M–5M at a time.
💵 Cash Exchange
Exchange USD at a gold shop the morning after you arrive in Da Nang — rates are significantly better than airport counters. Bills must be crisp and clean — ripped or marked bills get rejected.
🛍 Bargaining
At Ben Thanh and street markets, start at 40–50% of the asking price. Stay friendly and smile — it's a game, not a fight. Walk away slowly if they won't budge — they'll often call you back.
💳 Cards
Your Amex works at all 5-star hotel restaurants and reception. Use Grab with a card for rides. Many street vendors and markets are cash only — always keep ₫200K–500K on you.
